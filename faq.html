Accountancy, Business & Management Strand

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ABM strand, and what subjects does it cover?

The ABM strand stands for Accountancy, Business, and Management. It is one of the academic tracks offered in senior high school or college, focusing on business-related subjects. Common subjects in the ABM strand include Accounting, Business Mathematics, Principles of Marketing, Business Ethics and Social Responsibility, Business Finance, Entrepreneurship, and Human Resource Management, among others.

What is the ABM strand in senior high school?

The ABM strand is one of the four specialized tracks in the Philippines' K-12 education system, specifically designed for students interested in pursuing careers in the fields of accountancy, business, economics, finance, marketing, entrepreneurship, and management.

What are the career opportunities for ABM graduates?

ABM graduates have a wide range of career opportunities in various fields. Some of the common career paths include becoming accountants, financial analysts, marketing professionals, human resource managers, business consultants, and entrepreneurs.

Is the ABM strand only for those who want to pursue a career in business?

While the ABM strand primarily focuses on business-related subjects, it also provides a solid foundation in critical thinking, problem-solving, and communication skills, which are valuable in various careers, not just limited to business.

Can students from the ABM strand pursue other non-business courses in college?

Yes, students from the ABM strand can choose to pursue non-business courses in college. The strand equips them with essential skills and knowledge that can be applicable in different fields, enabling them to explore other academic paths if they wish.

Are there any prerequisites for choosing the ABM strand in senior high school?

Generally, there are no strict prerequisites for choosing the ABM strand in senior high school. However, students who have an interest in business, finance, economics, or management-related fields may find this strand more suitable for their career goals.
